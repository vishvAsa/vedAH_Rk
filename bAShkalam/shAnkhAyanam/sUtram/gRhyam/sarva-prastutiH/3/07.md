<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

गृहा मा बिभीत मा वेपध्वमूर्जं बिभ्रत एमसि ऊर्जं बिभ्रद्वः सुमनाः सुमेधा गृहानैमि मनसा मोदमानः येषामध्येति प्रवसन्येषु सौमनसो बहुः गृहानुप ह्वयामहे ते नो जानन्तु जानतः उपहूता इह गाव उपहूता अजावयः अथोऽन्नस्य कीलाल उपहूतो गृहेषु नः २

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

2 [^1] . 'House, do not fear, do not tremble; bringing strength we come back. Bringing strength, joyful and wise, I come back to thee, to the house, rejoicing in my mind.

[^1]:  7, 2. On kīlāla, see chap. 3, 1.

'That of which the traveller thinks, that in which dwells much joy, that I call the house. May it know us as we know it.

'Hither are called the cows; hither are called goats and sheep; and the sweet essence (?) of food is called hither to our house.'

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

5 [^2] . When accepting the water for washing the feet he says, 'The milk of Virāj art thou; may I obtain the milk of Virāj; in me (may) the milk of Padyā Virāj (dwell)!'

[^2]:  Padyā virāj is the Virāj metre, so far as it consists of p. 98 Pādas; in this connection, of course, the phrase is intended besides to convey the meaning of 'the splendour which dwells in the feet.' Comp. Pāraskara I, 3, 12 and Professor Stenzler's note there. My German translation of this Sūtra of Śāṅkhāyana rests on a misunderstanding.
