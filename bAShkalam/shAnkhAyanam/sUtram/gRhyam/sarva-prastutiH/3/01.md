<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

स्नानं समावर्त्स्यमानस्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

स्नानं समावर्त्स्यमानस्य

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

1. A bath (shall be taken by the student) when he is going to return home (from his teacher).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

आनडुहमित्युक्तं तस्मिन्नुपवेश्य केशश्मश्रूणि वापयति लोमनखानि च २
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

आनडुहमित्युक्तं तस्मिन्नुपवेश्य केशश्मश्रूणि वापयति लोमनखानि च २
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

2 [^1] . 'A bull's hide' - this has been declared. On that hide he makes him sit down and have his hair and beard cut and the hair of the body and the nails.

[^1]:  1, 2. Comp. above, I, 16, 1, and the note there.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

व्रीहियवैस्तिलसर्षपैरपामार्गैः सदापुष्पीभिरित्युद्वाप्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

व्रीहियवैस्तिलसर्षपैरपामार्गैः सदापुष्पीभिरित्युद्वाप्य

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

3. Having had (the cut-off hair-ends, &c.) thrown away together with rice and barley, with sesamum-seed and mustard-seed, with Apāmārga and Sadāpuṣpī flowers,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

आपोहिष्ठीयेनाभिषिच्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

आपोहिष्ठीयेनाभिषिच्य

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

4 [^2] . Having sprinkled him (with water) with the Āpohiṣṭhīyā-hymn (Rig-veda X, 9),

[^2]:  Nārāyaṇa says here, enaṃ māṇavakam abhishicya abhishekaṃ snānaṃ kārayitvā. Comp. Pāraskara II, 6, 9 seq.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अलङ्कृत्य ५
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अलङ्कृत्य ५
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

5. Having adorned him,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

युवं वस्त्राणीति वाससी परिधाय
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

युवं वस्त्राणीति वाससी परिधाय

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

6. Having dressed him with two garments with (the verse), 'The garments both of you' (Rig-veda I, 152, 1),

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अथास्मै निष्कं बध्नात्यायुष्यं वर्चस्यं ७
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अथास्मै निष्कं बध्नात्यायुष्यं वर्चस्यं ७
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

7. He then puts on him a golden ornament (with the words), 'Giving life and vigour' (Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā XXXIV, 50).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

ममाग्ने वर्च इति वेष्टनं ८
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

ममाग्ने वर्च इति वेष्टनं ८
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

8. With (the verse), 'Mine, Agni, be vigour' (Rig-veda X, 128, 1), the veiling (of the head is done).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

गृहङ्गृहमहनेति छत्त्रम् ९
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

गृहङ्गृहमहनेति छत्त्रम् ९
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

9. With (the verse), 'House by house the shining one' (Rig-veda I, 123, 4) (he takes) the parasol,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

आरोहतेत्युपानहौ १०
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

आरोहतेत्युपानहौ १०
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

10. With (the verse), 'Rise up' (Rig-veda X, 18, 6), the shoes,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

दीर्घस्ते अस्त्वङ्कुश इति वैणवं दण्डमादत्ते ११
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

दीर्घस्ते अस्त्वङ्कुश इति वैणवं दण्डमादत्ते ११
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

11. With (the verse), 'Long be thy hook' (Rig-veda VIII, 17, 10) he takes a bamboo staff.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

प्रतिलीनस्तदहरासीत १२
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

प्रतिलीनस्तदहरासीत १२
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

12 [^3] . Let him sit that day in solitude.

[^3]:  Pratilīna evidently means the same thing that is so often expressed in the Buddhist texts by paṭisallīna.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

वनस्पते वीड्वङ्गः शास इत्थेति रथमारोहेद् १३
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

वनस्पते वीड्वङ्गः शास इत्थेति रथमारोहेद् १३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

13. With (the verses), 'O tree! with strong limbs,' and, 'A ruler indeed' (Rig-veda VI, 47, 26; X, 152, 1) let him mount the chariot.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

यत्रैनं गवा वा पशुना वा अर्हयेयुस्तत्पूर्वमुपतिष्ठेत १४
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

यत्रैनं गवा वा पशुना वा अर्हयेयुस्तत्पूर्वमुपतिष्ठेत १४
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

14. (Before returning home) let him first approach a place where they will perform Argha for him with a cow or a goat.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

गोभ्यो वा समावर्तेत फलवतो वा वृक्षाद् १५
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

गोभ्यो वा समावर्तेत फलवतो वा वृक्षाद् १५
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

15. Or let him return (making his start) from cows or from a fruit-bearing tree.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

इन्द्र श्रेष्ठानि द्रविणानि धेहि  
स्योना पृथिवि भवेत्यवरोहति
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

इन्द्र श्रेष्ठानि द्रविणानि धेहि  
स्योना पृथिवि भवेत्यवरोहति 

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

16. With (the verses), 'Indra, give us best goods,' and, 'Be friendly, O earth' (Rig-veda II, 21, 6; I, 22, 15) he descends (from the chariot).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

ईप्सितमन्नं तदहर्भुञ्जीत
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

ईप्सितमन्नं तदहर्भुञ्जीत

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

17. Let him eat that day his favourite food.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

आचार्याय वस्त्रयुगं दद्यादुष्णीषं मणिकुण्डलं दण्डोपानहं छत्त्रं च १८
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

आचार्याय वस्त्रयुगं दद्यादुष्णीषं मणिकुण्डलं दण्डोपानहं छत्त्रं च १८
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

18 [^4] . To his teacher he shall give (that) pair of garments, the turban, ear-rings and jewel, staff and shoes, and the parasol.

[^4]:  The pair of garments are those referred to in Sūtra 6; on the turban see Sūtra 8. On staff and shoes comp. Sūtras 10, 11; on the parasol, Sūtra 9.

</details>
