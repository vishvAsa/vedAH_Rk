अनाहिताग्निर्नवं प्राशिष्यन्नाग्रयणदेवताभ्यः स्विष्टकृच्चतुर्थीभ्यः स्वाहाकारेण गृह्येऽग्नौ जुहुयात् १
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अनाहिताग्निर्नवं प्राशिष्यन्नाग्रयणदेवताभ्यः स्विष्टकृच्चतुर्थीभ्यः स्वाहाकारेण गृह्येऽग्नौ जुहुयात् १
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

1 [^1] . When one who has not set up the (sacred Śrauta) fires, is going to partake of the first-fruits (of the harvest), let him sacrifice to the Āgrayaṇa deities with (Agni) Sviṣṭakṛt as the fourth, and with the word SVĀHĀ, on his (sacred) domestic fire.

[^1]:  8, 1. The Āgrayaṇeṣṭi is the corresponding rite of the Śrauta ritual. Comp. Indische Studien, X, 343. The deities of that sacrifice are Indra and Agni; the Viśve devās; Heaven and Earth.

</details>

2. Having recited over (the food) which he is going to eat (the formula), 'To Prajāpati I draw thee, the proper portion, for luck to me, for glory to me, for food to me!' -

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

3 [^2] . He thrice eats of it, sprinkling it with water, with (the verse), 'From the good you have led us to the better, ye gods! Through thee, the nouṛṣment, may we obtain thee. Thus enter into us, O potion, bringing refreshment; be a saviour to us, to men and animals!'

[^2]:  In the text read for tvayā jvasena, tvayāऽvasena.

</details>

4. With (the verse), 'This art thou, breath; the truth I speak This art thou; from all directions thou hast entered (into all beings). Thou driving away old age and sickness from my body be at home with me. Do not forsake us, Indra!' - he touches the place of the heart;

</details>
