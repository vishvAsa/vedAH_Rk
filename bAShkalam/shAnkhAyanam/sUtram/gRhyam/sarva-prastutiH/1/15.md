<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

1. The three verses, 'I loosen thee' (Rig-veda X, 85, 24), when she departs from the house.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

जीवं रुदन्तीति प्ररुदन्त्याम् २
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

2. 'The living one they bewail' (Rig-veda X, 40, 10), if she begins to cry.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अथ रथाक्षस्योपाञ्जनं पत्नी कुरुतेऽक्षन्नमीमदन्तेत्येतया सर्पिषा ३
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अथ रथाक्षस्योपाञ्जनं पत्नी कुरुतेऽक्षन्नमीमदन्तेत्येतया सर्पिषा ३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

3 [^1] . The wife then smears the axle of the chariot with clarified butter with this (verse), 'They feasted, they got drunk' (Rig-veda I, 82, 2),

[^1]:  15, 3. Probably the use of this verse on this occasion rests on the assonance of its opening word akṣan and akṣa (rathākṣa).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

शुची ते चक्रे द्वे ते चक्रे इति चैताभ्यां चक्रयोः पूर्वया पूर्वमुत्तरयोत्तरम् ४
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

शुची ते चक्रे द्वे ते चक्रे इति चैताभ्यां चक्रयोः पूर्वया पूर्वमुत्तरयोत्तरम् ४
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

4. And with the two (verses), 'Pure are thy wheels,' 'Thy two wheels' (Rig-veda X, 85, 12. 16), of the two wheels the first with the first (verse) and the second with the second (verse),

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

उस्रौ च ५
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

उस्रौ च ५
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

5. And the two bulls.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

खे रथस्येत्येतया फलवतो वृक्षस्य शम्यागर्तेष्वेकैकां वयां निखाय ६
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

खे रथस्येत्येतया फलवतो वृक्षस्य शम्यागर्तेष्वेकैकां वयां निखाय ६
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

6 [^2] . After (the wife?) has put, with this (verse), 'In the box of the wheel' (Rig-veda VIII, 80, 7), a branch of a fruit-bearing tree into each of the holes destined for the pins,

[^2]:  See Nārāyaṇa's note on śamyāgarta, p. 129 of the German edition.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

नित्या वाभिमन्त्र्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

नित्या वाभिमन्त्र्य 
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

7. Or, if (such branches) are (already) fixed, has recited (that verse) over them,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अथोस्रौ युञ्जन्ति युक्तस्ते अस्तु दक्षिण इति द्वाभ्यां शुक्रावनड्वाहावित्येतेनार्धर्चेन युक्तावभिमन्त्र्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अथोस्रौ युञ्जन्ति युक्तस्ते अस्तु दक्षिण इति द्वाभ्यां शुक्रावनड्वाहावित्येतेनार्धर्चेन युक्तावभिमन्त्र्य

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

8. They then harness the two bulls with the two (verses), 'Harnessed be thy right one' (Rig-veda I, 82, 5-6), (the bridegroom) reciting the half-verse, 'White the two bulls' (Rig-veda X, 85, 10), over them when they have been harnessed.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अथ यदि रथाङ्गं विशीर्येत छिद्येत वाहिताग्नेर्गृहान्कन्यां प्रपाद्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अथ यदि रथाङ्गं विशीर्येत छिद्येत वाहिताग्नेर्गृहान्कन्यां प्रपाद्य 
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

9. Now should any part of the chariot break or burst, let him take the girl to the house of one who keeps the sacred fires,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अभि व्ययस्व खदिरस्येत्येतया प्रतिदध्यात् १०
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अभि व्ययस्व खदिरस्येत्येतया प्रतिदध्यात् १०
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

10. And repair (the damage) with the verse, 'Cover thyself with the Khadiras' (Rig-veda III, 53, 19).

</details>

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

</details>

</details>
