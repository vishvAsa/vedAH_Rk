<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अथ जातकर्म १
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

1 [^1] . Now the Gātakarman (i.e. ceremony for the new-born child).

[^1]:  24, 1. Comp. Dr. Speijer's essay on the Jātakarman (Leiden, 1872). Nārāyaṇa observes that, as it is prescribed below (chap. 25, 4) that a mess of food is to be cooked in the sūtikāgni, here the sūtikāgni is established, and sacrifice is performed therein. The Sūtra I, 25, 4, from which it is to be inferred that the sūtikāgni should be kept, is considered, accordingly, as a Jñāpaka (see Professor Bühler's notes on Āpastamba I, II, 7; Gautama p. 50 I, 31; Nārāyaṇa's note on chap. 25, 4, p. 133 of the German edition).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

जातं कुमारं त्रिरभ्यवान्यानुप्राण्याद् ऋचा प्राणिहि यजुषा समनिहि साम्नोदनिहीति २
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

जातं कुमारं त्रिरभ्यवान्यानुप्राण्याद् ऋचा प्राणिहि यजुषा समनिहि साम्नोदनिहीति २
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

2 [^2] . Let (the father) breathe three times on the new-born child and then draw in his breath with the words, 'Draw in your breath with the Ṛc, breathe within with the Yajus, breathe forth with the Sāman.'

[^2]:  Abhyavānya should be corrected into abhyapānya, as in IV, 18, 1 nearly all the MSS. read nivāta instead of nipāta. The Śāmbavya MS. reads in the text, trir abhyānyānuprāṇya; in the commentary trir aṇyapāṇyānuprāṇyā. Comp., on the terminology of the different vital airs, Speijer, Jātakarma, p. 64 seq.; Eggeling, S.B.E., vol. xii, p. 20.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

सर्पिर्मधुनी दध्युदके च सन्निनीय व्रीहियवौ वा सन्निघृष्य त्रिः प्राशयेज्जातरूपेण ३
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सर्पिर्मधुनी दध्युदके च सन्निनीय व्रीहियवौ वा सन्निघृष्य त्रिः प्राशयेज्जातरूपेण ३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

3. Let him mix together butter and honey, milk curds and water, or grind together rice and barley, and give it to eat (to the child) thrice from gold (i.e. from a golden vessel or with a golden spoon),

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

प्र ते यच्छामि मधुमन्मखाय वेदं प्रसूतं सवित्रा मघोना । आयुष्मान्गुपितो देवताभिः शतं जीव शरदो लोके अस्मिन्नित्यसाविति नामास्य दधाति घोषवदाद्यन्तरन्तस्थं द्व्यक्षरं चतुरक्षरं वापि वा षळक्षरं कृतं कुर्यान्न तद्धितं ४
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

प्र ते यच्छामि मधुमन्मखाय वेदं प्रसूतं सवित्रा मघोना । आयुष्मान्गुपितो देवताभिः शतं जीव शरदो लोके अस्मिन्नित्यसाविति नामास्य दधाति घोषवदाद्यन्तरन्तस्थं द्व्यक्षरं चतुरक्षरं वापि वा षळक्षरं कृतं कुर्यान्न तद्धितं ४
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

4. With (the verse), 'I administer to thee honey food for the festival, the wisdom ("veda") raised by Savitar the bountiful; long-living, protected by the gods, live a hundred autumns in this world, N.N.!'(with these words) he gives him a name beginning with a sonant, with a semivowel in it, consisting of two syllables, or of four syllables, or also of six syllables; he should take a kṛt (suffix), not a taddhita.

</details>

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

गोः कृष्णस्य शुक्लकृष्णानि लोहितानि च रोमाणि मषं कारयित्वैतस्मिन्नेव चतुष्टये सन्निनीय चतुः प्राशयेदिति माण्डूकेयः

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

गोः कृष्णस्य शुक्लकृष्णानि लोहितानि च रोमाणि मषं कारयित्वैतस्मिन्नेव चतुष्टये सन्निनीय चतुः प्राशयेदिति माण्डूकेयः 
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

7. Let him pulverise black and white and red hairs of a black ox, intermix (that powder) with those four substances (see Sūtra 3), and give it to eat (to the child) four times: such (is the opinion of) Māṇḍūkeya.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

भूरृग्वेदं त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहा भुवो यजुर्वेदं त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहा स्वः सामवेदं त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहा भूर्भुवः स्वर्वाकोवाक्यमितिहासपुराणमॐ सर्वान्वेदाँ स्त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहेति वा ८
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

भूरृग्वेदं त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहा भुवो यजुर्वेदं त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहा स्वः सामवेदं त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहा भूर्भुवः स्वर्वाकोवाक्यमितिहासपुराणमॐ सर्वान्वेदाँ स्त्वयि दधाम्यसौ स्वाहेति वा ८
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

8 [^3] . If he likes (let him do so) with the words, 'Bhūḥ! The Rig-veda I lay into thee, N.N., svāhā!

[^3]:  Veti vikalpārthe. bhūr ṛgvedam ityādicaturbhir mantrair asāv ity atra pūrveva (read pūrvavat?) kumāranāmagrahaṇapūrvakaṃ kumāraṃ prāśayet. Nārāyaṇa.

'Bhuvaḥ! The Yajur-veda I lay into thee, N.N., svāhā!

'Svaḥ! The Sāma-veda I lay into thee, N.N., svāhā!

'Bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ! Vākovākya (colloquies), Itihāsa, and Purāṇa - Om! All the Vedas I lay into thee, N.N., svāhā!'

</details>

9. The production of intelligence (is performed) by thrice saying in his right ear, 'Speech!'

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

वाग्देवी मनसा संविदाना प्राणेन वत्सेन सहेन्द्र प्रोक्ता जुषतां त्वा सौमनसाय देवी मही मन्द्रा वाणी वाणीची सलिला स्वयम्भूरिति चानुमन्त्रयेत १०
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

10. And let him recite over (the child the following text), 'Speech, the goddess, united with mind, together with breath, the child, uttered by Indra - may she rejoice in thee, the goddess, for the sake of joy, the great one, the sweet sounding, the music, full of music, the flowing, self-produced.'

</details>
