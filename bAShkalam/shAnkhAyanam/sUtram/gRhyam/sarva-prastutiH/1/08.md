</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अथ परिस्तरणं १
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

1 [^1] . Now (follows) the strewing (of grass) around (the fire).

[^1]:  8, 1. Comp. the passages quoted in Professor Eggeling's note on Śatapatha Br. I, 1, 1, 22.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

प्रागग्रैः कुशैः परिस्तृणाति त्रिवृत्पञ्चवृद्वा २
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

प्रागग्रैः कुशैः परिस्तृणाति त्रिवृत्पञ्चवृद्वा २
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

2. He strews eastward-pointed Kuśa grass around it, in three layers or in five layers,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

पुरस्तात्प्रथममथ पश्चादथ पश्चान् ३
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

पुरस्तात्प्रथममथ पश्चादथ पश्चान् ३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

3. Beginning on the east side, then to the west, then to the west.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

मूलान्यग्रैः प्रच्छादयति ४
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

मूलान्यग्रैः प्रच्छादयति ४
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

4. He covers the roots (of the grass-blades) with the points.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

सर्वाश्चावृतो दक्षिणतःप्रवृत्तय उदक्संस्था भवन्ति ५
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सर्वाश्चावृतो दक्षिणतःप्रवृत्तय उदक्संस्था भवन्ति ५
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

5. And all kinds of rites are to be performed beginning south, ending north.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

दक्षिणतो ब्रह्माणं प्रतिष्ठाप्य भूर्भुवः स्वरिति ६
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

दक्षिणतो ब्रह्माणं प्रतिष्ठाप्य भूर्भुवः स्वरिति ६
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

6 [^2] . He places the Brahman south with the words, BHŪR BHUVAḤ SVAḤ,

[^2]:  Ordinarily there was no real Brahman present, and his place was filled by a bundle of Kuśa grass that represented him. Nārāyaṇa states that this bundle should consist of fifty blades of Kuśa grass. Comp. also the Gṛhya-saṃgraha-pariśiṣṭa I, 89-90.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

सुमनोभिरलङ्कृत्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सुमनोभिरलङ्कृत्य
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

7. Adorns him with flowers,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

उत्तरतः प्रणीताः प्रणीय को वः प्रणयतीति ७
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

उत्तरतः प्रणीताः प्रणीय को वः प्रणयतीति ७
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

8 [^3] . Carries forward on the north side the Praṇītā waters with the words, 'Who carries ye forward?' - 

[^3]:  Comp. the passages quoted by Dr. Bloomfield, Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenländ. Gesellschaft, vol. xxxv, p. 565, note 2.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

सव्येन कुशानादाय दक्षिणेनापनौति ९
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सव्येन कुशानादाय दक्षिणेनापनौति ९
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

9 [^4] . Takes up with the left hand the Kuśa blades, and arranges them (on the ground) with the right hand,

[^4]:  This Sūtra shows that the paristaraṇa, though already treated of in Sūtras 1-4, is not to be performed till after the 'carrying forward' of the Praṇītā water. Comp. Nārāyaṇa's note on Sūtra 1 (p. 123 of the German edition). That this is indeed the order of the different acts is confirmed by Pāraskara I, 1, 2.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

दक्षिणं जान्वाच्य १०
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

दक्षिणं जान्वाच्य १०
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

10. Bending his right knee,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

सव्यं पित्र्ये ११
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सव्यं पित्र्ये ११
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

11. The left when worshipping the Manes.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

नाज्याहुतिषु नित्यं परिस्तरणं १२
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

नाज्याहुतिषु नित्यं परिस्तरणं १२
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

12. The strewing around (of the grass) is not necessary in the Ājya offerings,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

नित्याहुतिषु चेति माण्डूकेयः १३
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

नित्याहुतिषु चेति माण्डूकेयः १३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

13 [^5] . Nor in the standing offerings, according to Māṇḍūkeya.

[^5]:  'In the standing offerings, such as the Vaiśvadeva sacrifice in the morning and in the evening.' Nārāyaṇa.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

कुशतरुणे अविषमे अविच्छिन्नाग्रे अनन्तर्गर्भे प्रादेशेन मापयित्वा कुशेन च्छिनत्ति पवित्रे स्थ इति १४
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

कुशतरुणे अविषमे अविच्छिन्नाग्रे अनन्तर्गर्भे प्रादेशेन मापयित्वा कुशेन च्छिनत्ति पवित्रे स्थ इति १४
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

14 [^6] . He now measures off with the span (of his hand) two Kuśa blades, which are not unequal, with unbroken points, bearing no young shoots in them, and severs them (from their roots) with a Kuśa blade, saying, 'Purifiers are ye.'

[^6]:  Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā I, 12 a.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

द्वे त्रीणि वा भवन्ति १५
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

द्वे त्रीणि वा भवन्ति १५
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

15. There are two or three (of these Kuśa strainers).

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

प्रागग्रे धारयन्वैष्णव्यावित्यभ्युक्ष्य १६
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

प्रागग्रे धारयन्वैष्णव्यावित्यभ्युक्ष्य १६
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

16. He holds them with their points to the east and sprinkles them (with water, saying), 'Belonging to Viṣṇu.'

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

कुशतरुणाभ्यां प्रदक्षिणमग्निं त्रिः पर्युक्ष्य १७
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

कुशतरुणाभ्यां प्रदक्षिणमग्निं त्रिः पर्युक्ष्य १७
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

17. With the two Kuśa blades he sprinkles (water) around the fire three times, keeping his right side turned towards it,

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

महीनां पयोऽसीत्याज्यस्थालीमादाय
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

महीनां पयोऽसीत्याज्यस्थालीमादाय

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

18 [^7] . Takes up the Ājya pot with the words, 'Milk of the cows art thou;'

[^7]:  Vāj. Saṃh. IV, 3 a.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

इषे त्वेत्यधिश्रित्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

इषे त्वेत्यधिश्रित्य

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

19 [^8] . Puts it on the fire with the words, 'For sap thee;'

[^8]:  Vāj. Saṃh. I, 22 d.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

उर्जे त्वेत्युदगुद्वास्य
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

उर्जे त्वेत्युदगुद्वास्य

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

20 [^9] . Takes it from the fire towards the north with the words, 'For juice thee;'

[^9]:  Vāj. Saṃh. I, 30 c.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

उदगग्रे पवित्रे धारयन्नङ्गुष्ठाभ्यां चोपकनिष्ठिकाभ्यां चोभयतः प्रतिगृह्योर्ध्वाग्रे प्रह्वे कृत्वाज्ये प्रत्यस्यति सवितुष्ट्वा प्रसव उत्पुनाम्यच्छिद्रे ण पवित्रेण वसोः सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिरिति
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

उदगग्रे पवित्रे धारयन्नङ्गुष्ठाभ्यां चोपकनिष्ठिकाभ्यां चोभयतः प्रतिगृह्योर्ध्वाग्रे प्रह्वे कृत्वाज्ये प्रत्यस्यति सवितुष्ट्वा प्रसव उत्पुनाम्यच्छिद्रे ण पवित्रेण वसोः सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिरिति 

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

21 [^10] . And holding the two (Kuśa) strainers with their points to the north, seizing them on both sides with his two thumbs and fourth fingers, he bends them down, the points upwards, and dips them into the Ājya with the words,

[^10]:  Vāj. Saṃh. I, 12 b. - The division of Sūtras 21 and 22 should be after iti, not, as the Indian tradition has it, after raśmibhiḥ.

'By the impulse of Savitar I purify thee with this uninjured purifier, with the rays of the good sun.'

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

आज्यसंस्कारः सर्वत्र २२

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

22. (This) preparation of the Ājya (takes place) each time.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

नासंस्कृतेन जुहुयात् २३

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

23. Let him not offer (Ājya) which has not been (thus) prepared.

</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

24 [^11] . Also the waters in the Sruva spoon (he purifies) with the words, '(By the impulse) of Savitar (I purify) you.'

[^11]:  24, 25. Rāmacandra: 'He pours water into the Sruva and purifies this also, as he had done with the Ājya (Sūtra 21) . . . . He then pours a little portion of that water on to the Praṇītā water (see above, Sūtra 8), and with the rest, which is called the Prokṣaṇī water, he sprinkles the sacrificial food, the fuel, and the Barhis.'

</details>

25. This (is called) the PRAṆĪTĀ and the PROKṢAṆĪ water.
