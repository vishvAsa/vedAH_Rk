The тАШgoadтАЩ (tod├б-) found in VI.6.6 reappears here, as well as in vs. 3. Thus, 3 of the 5 occurrences of this word in the RV are found in this Agni cycle. In VI.6.6 I argue that the referent of the тАЬgreat goadтАЭ is the sun (see comm. ad loc., sim. I.150.1).

Oldenberg (ZDMG 55.301, also Noten) thinks the sun is the referent in our passage as well, and, further, he construes the gen. t├│dasya in p─Бda b with ┼Ыoc├нс╣г─Б tat─Бna in p─Бda d, on the basis of VI.6.6 bh─Бn├║n─Б тАж tod├бsya тАж tatantha. Although the parallel is suggestive, the distance between the genitive and its supposed governing instr. in our passage seems too far, esp. since the 2nd hemistich begins ay├бm s├б тАЬhere is heтАЭ or тАЬthis one here,тАЭ a sequence that seems to open a new (though co-referential) clause.

Moreover, in vs. 3 tod├б- seems to refer to or be compared to Agni himself, and so the internal evidence of the hymn favors a connection of the goad with Agni, not directly with the sun. I therefore follow Geldner in taking tod├бsya as dependent on r─Б╠Бс╣н, which also governs barh├нс╣гaс╕е. It may be that r─Б╠Бс╣н тАж tod├бsya тАЬruler of the goadтАЭ is a phrase like s┼лn├║с╕е s├бhasaс╕е тАЬson of strengthтАЭ (see 1c), where тАЬson of XтАЭ is tantamount to X. Both the sun (тАЬthe great goadтАЭ) and Agni are goads because with their appearance at dawn they set the world in motion. Since Agni is often taken as an earthly form of the sun, sharing the same third party identity would not be surprising, with Agni being the lesser of the two by nature.

For Agni as тАЬruler of the ritual grass,тАЭ see VIII.13.4=15.5 … asy├б barh├нс╣гo v├н r─Бjasi, though the subject there is Indra. I take y├бjadhyai as a predicated infinitive (sim. Geldner, Renou, Keydana [Inf., 171]).

Jamison Brereton Notes

The first hemistich is partly assembled from material also found elsewhere: the quite straightforward 2nd p─Бda is identical to VII.12.2b. The post-caesura portion of the first p─Бda, et├бr─л n├б ┼Ы┼лс╣гa├нс╕е, is also found at V.41.10, where the pre-caesura portion, gс╣Ыс╣З─лt├й agn├нс╕е тАЬAgni is sungтАЭ, is functionally identical to our 2nd p─Бda (esp.

agn├н с╣гс╣нave тАЬAgni is praisedтАЭ). On et├бr─л as a loc., see comm. ad V.41.10; note that this word is a partial anagram of 3a vaner─Бс╣н.

In c note the insistent phonetic figure: dr(├║)vanno vanv├бn kr├бt(u)v─Б n├б ├бrv─Б.

The interpr. of p─Бda d is difficult because of the highly unusual form j─Бray─Б╠Бyi, which has been much discussed (see esp. Oldenberg ZDMG 55.302-3). Since the hemistich otherwise lacks a verb form, it is tempting to see a verb here. But the accent makes trouble because this is a main clause with no syntactic break evident before the word.

Nonetheless, it is generally taken as a nonce aor. passive and quite possibly a punning one: as a denom. to j─Бr├б- тАШwooer, loverтАЩ (hence тАШbecome a wooerтАЩ) and as a pass. built to the caus. j─Бr├бyati (/ jar├бyati) тАШawakenтАЩ. The pun is most clearly expressed in GeldnerтАЩs tr. тАЬwie der Vater des Uс╣гas zum Buhlen ward, so wurde er durch die Opfer erwecktтАЭ; he takes it as referring to the myth of incest of Heaven, also signalled by the phrase usr├бс╕е pit├йva тАЬlike the father of Dawn.тАЭ Although I am always game (perhaps too game) to see puns everywhere in the RV, I am dubious about the one suggested here. For one thing the somewhat anomalous stem uс╣г├бr-/usr- is never used for personified Dawn, but only for the temporal dawn. (For supposed voc. uс╣гar in I.49.4 see comm. ad loc. and Lundquist 2014.) It seems unlikely that the stem typed for the goddess, uс╣г├бs-, would not be used in this myth where her identity is so very crucial. Moreover, I rather doubt that usr├бс╕е here is a gen. sg. with pit├йva. Not only is the simile particle wrongly placed (though this is not rare), but usr├бs is almost always an acc. pl., which can express extent of time (e.g., VII.15.8). The solution I favor for j─Бray─Б╠Бyi is one also mentioned by Oldenberg, stemming from Ludwig, and endorsed by Debrunner in the Nachtr. to AiG I [p. 163] – that it belong to a gerundive stem j─Бray─Б╠Бy(i)ya- тАШto be awakenedтАЩ and the expected nom. sg. * j─Бray─Б╠Бy(i)yaс╕е lost its final syllable by haplology before yaj├▒a├нс╕е, not surprisingly in this y(a)-rich environment. This gerundive is predicated and serve in lieu of a finite verb. For a similarly formed predicated gerundive, see nearby trayay─Б╠Бy(i)yaс╕е at VI.2.7.
