The various, and sometimes paradoxical, relationships between (masculine) Heaven and (feminine) Earth, and their joint relationship with their sons, the gods, are the topic of this hymn. The two are named in the first and last (5) verses of the hymn, while in between they are only referred to by kinship terms. In the meantime, the beloved paradox of children giving birth to their parents is given full play in verses 3 and 4.
