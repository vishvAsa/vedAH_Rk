Jamison Brereton Notes

Note the unaccented asmin in p─Бda a, referring to Soma, who is the default referent even without a previous mention in the hymn.

The vs. contains three similes, each of which presents at least some interpretational challenges to the audience. The first two are in ab and match the frame тАЬthe thoughts contend over himтАЭ (тАж asmin тАж sp├бrdhante dh├нyaс╕е), which preumably refers to the thoughts produced by poets at different and competing rituals (see Ober I.407 and n. 64). The second simile, in b, is the easier to interpret: тАЬlike clans over the sunтАЭ (s┼л╠Бrye n├б v├н┼Ыaс╕е). Like the competing thoughts that each seek to appropriate Soma, different clans all seek to secure their place in the sun, a symbol, acdg. to Ober (I.457), of Leben and Lebenskraft, of Lebensraum.

The first simile, v─Бj├нn─лva ┼Ы├║bhaс╕е, reads slightly askew. It should mean тАЬas adornments (contend over) a prizewinnerтАЭ - but what would that really mean? The passages adduced by Geldner in n. 1a are not helpful, and no one else that I know of attempts to elucidate it. I think the poet has deliberately misdirected us. To begin with, although the loc. v─Бj├нni appears to match asmin in the frame as the object of contention, I think that may not be the case or may not only be the case. The vs. begins with ├бdhi, which therefore appears to be in tmesis with sp├бrdhante in b, but there is only one other instance of ├бdhi тИЪspс╣Ыdh in the RV (VI.34.1, where - I must admit - it seems to have the sense attributed to our passage: тАШcontend overтАЩ with loc. ├нndre as the object of contention). The word ├бdhi is more often an adposition, most commonly with the loc., and so I think it is here. Although ├бdhi is separated from v─Бj├нni, what intervenes is WackernagelтАЩs position material: subordinating y├бd, which frequently takes 2nd position, and the enclitic asmin, which would lean upon it. So effectively ├бdhi тАж v─Бj├нni can be a prepositional phrase interrupted by the interpolation of those two WackernagelтАЩs position words. Under this interpr. the prizewinner is no longer the object of contention but the locus of it. Now as to ┼Ы├║bhaс╕е: this root noun is quite well attested (over 40 occurrences, incl. the common voc. ┼Ыubh├бs p├бti-), but only two attestations are plural - our passage and V.54.11, which describes the many appurenances and adornments found on the Maruts and their equipage, incl. p─Бda b v├бkс╣гassu rukm─Б╠Бmaruto r├бthe ┼Ы├║bhaс╕е тАЬ your breasts brilliants, o Maruts, on your chariot charmsтАЭ (per the published translation). The ┼Ы├║bhaс╕е here are ornaments of some sort, quite possibly sparkly or otherwise eye-catching, that jazz up the chariot to which theyтАЩre affixed. I suggest that our loc. (├бdhi тАж) v─Бj├нni fulfills the same function as r├бthe in V.54.11, and that the ┼Ы├║bhaс╕е in our passage are not vying over or for the prizewinner, but, located on him, they are vying with each other to best catch the eye of observers.

The phrase that opens the 2nd hemistich, ap├│ vс╣Ыс╣З─Бn├бс╕е тАЬchoosing the waters,тАЭ is a little odd. The waters are surely the ubiquitous waters for mixing found regularly in IX, but why would Soma тАЬchooseтАЭ them? In a soma context we would expect rather ap├│ *vas─Бn├бс╕е тАЬclothing himself in the watersтАЭ; this exact phrase opens the p─Бda in IX.78.1, 86.40, 96.13, 107.4, 18, 26, and with acc. sg. of the participle IX.16.2, 109.21. I suggest that our poet is knowingly playing on this standard formula, using a different root but identical formation to throw the expression offkilter. Note that v├бs─Бna- (nom. pl.) is found in the same metrical position in 4c. The identical expression, ap├│ vс╣Ыс╣З─Бn├бс╕е, is, however, found in V.48.1; on this opaque passage see comm. ad loc. Interpretation of the third simile in the vs. is complicated by the fact that it is unclear which part of the clause to construe it with. The frame consists of an acc. m├бnma тАШthoughtтАЩ (├а тАШpoemтАЩ), which is compared to vraj├бс╣Г n├б pa┼Ыuv├бrdhan─Бya тАЬa stable for raising livestock.тАЭ But where these acc. expressions fit in the sentence is disputed: Geldner (see n. 1d; also Tichy, dvit─Б╠Б222 = KlSch 213) takes m├бnma as a second obj. with vс╣Ыс╣З─Бn├бс╕е. But the simile then makes little sense: although Soma might well тАЬchoose a thought,тАЭ choosing a stable is a different proposition. By this interpr. the domain of the comparison would only be the acc. m├бnma; it could not fit with the verb (despite TichyтАЩs odd тАЬwie (man) eine H├╝rde тАж [w├дhlt]тАЭ).
