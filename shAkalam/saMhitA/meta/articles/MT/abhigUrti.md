The word abhigUrti/abhigUrta is mentioned only in the ashvamedha sUkta of the R^igveda 3 times. Indologists generally translate it as praise or applause. However, the fact that it is used only in this sUkta and nowhere else hints that it had a more specific meaning. We wonder if it was a specific chant deployed in the ashvamedha. As a parallel, the yajurveda has a chant known as the svayamabhigUrta (seen by interpreters as self-approval) offered to a series of recipients from the divine soma-bearing eagle to father manu.
