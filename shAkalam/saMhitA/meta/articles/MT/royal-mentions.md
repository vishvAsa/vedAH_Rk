The most commonly mentioned kings of the R^igveda: 

## divodAsa
divodAsa: 18  
atithigva: 12

These two epithets refer to the same ruler; hence, the total mentions of divodAsa atithigva sum to 30. He is a dominant figure in maNDala 6. His son paruchChepa is rAjarShi in maNDala 1.

## sudAs: 27

He is mostly mentioned only in maNDala 7, where he is patron, protagonist, and contemporary hero.

## tR^itsu-s: 9

Some interpret this as the clan of sudAs and others as that of vasiShTha. They are solely mentioned in maNDala 7 by vasiShTha, the composer of the said maNDala, or his brother agastya. We suspect they are not a royal clan at all, but the original family name of the maitravaruNa-s before the claimed divine epithet came to be attached to them.

## turvasha-s: 22

This refers to the king turvasha and his successors; one of the pa~nchajana or the five peoples.

## yadu-s: 17
This refers to the king yadu and his successors; one of the pa~nchajana or the five peoples.

## druhyu-s: 6
This refers to the druhyu and his successors; one of the pa~nchajana or the five peoples.

## anu-s: 4
This refers to anu and his successors; one of the pa~nchajana or the five peoples.

## pUru-s: 20
This refers to the king pUru and his successors; one of the pa~nchajana or the five peoples.

## trasadasyu
trasadasyu: 13
purukutsa: 5
He is the father of trasadasyu.

## kutsa 17
Arjuneya 1
kutsa is surnamed Arjuneya; so he is mentioned a total of 18 times and likely the same as the author of the eponymous upamaNDala in maNDala 1.

## Legacy
While the occupants of the royal seats of these ancient rulers are still major players in the epics, the heroes of the veda like divodAsa, sudAs, kutsa, trasadasyu, purukutsa are at best faintly remembered. This alone should indicate to any sane person that the two were vastly removed in time.+++(5)+++
