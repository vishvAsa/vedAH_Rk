<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

For they say about one who is repeating with his speech (the words of another), “I have stopped his talkativeness (*śarmavat*).”[22]
</details>

<details><summary>English - Notes</summary>

[22]The words, शर्मवदास्मा[[??]]पयांसि are no doubt an idiomatical phrase of the ancient Saṅskrit, the exact meaning of which it is now impossible to determine, Sâyaṇa explains it in the following way: अस्मैगुरोक्तार्थस्य सम्यगनुवादिने शिष्याय शर्मवत् सुखयुक्तजीवनं संपन्नं । यस्मात् तस्मात् । हे शिष्य [[??]] सि समन्ततो नियतो ऽस्मि The irregular form आस्ना instead of अस्मै he takes a Vedic anomaly. The phrase, he further adds, is applied in common life when one's speech is stopped. The author of the Brâhmaṇa adduces this phrase only in illustration of the supposed identity of *Vâch* with *Sarma*.
</details>
